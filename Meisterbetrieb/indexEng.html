Step by step

From first call to warm home

We combine clear planning with clean installation. Every project follows the same structure, so you know exactly where you are in the process and what happens next.

Step 1 Site visit & calculation

On-site survey, heat load & honest consultation

We visit your home, check the building, radiators and existing system and calculate the heat demand. This shows whether a heat pump really makes sense for your property.

  • Measurement of living area, building shell and radiator surfaces
  • Estimation of heat losses and required heat output
  • First cost range and realistic savings expectations
Step 2 Planning & offer

System design, funding & fixed-price offer

We select the right heat pump, design hydraulics and buffering and check which KfW funding is realistic for your case.

  • Selection of manufacturer and output size
  • Hydraulic concept incl. buffer / hydraulic balance
  • Transparent offer with optional services and KfW options
Step 3 Installation

Clean, coordinated installation on site

We coordinate scaffolders and electricians if needed, protect your rooms and install the system with as little disruption as possible.

  • Delivery of heat pump and all components
  • Hydraulic connection, flushing and leak test
  • Electrical connection and communication setup
Step 4 Handover & service

Fine-tuning, handover & long-term support

We adjust the system to your habits, explain the controls and remain your first point of contact for questions, maintenance and optimisation.

  • System settings for comfort and efficiency
  • Explanation of controls and app (if available)
  • Optional maintenance and service agreement

FAQ

Questions homeowners often ask us

Most of our clients are not heating engineers – and they don’t need to be. Here are some of the questions we hear again and again before a project starts.

Old buildings Noise Running costs Subsidies

Can’t find your question here? Just send us a short message via the contact form – we will gladly give you a straight answer before you decide.

Do heat pumps also work in older Berlin buildings?

In many older buildings a heat pump is possible – but not automatically. We look at the radiators, flow temperatures, insulation level and your existing boiler. If the building needs very high temperatures to stay warm, we either improve efficiency (for example by larger radiators) or tell you honestly if a heat pump would not be the right choice yet.

How loud is the outdoor unit and will my neighbours hear it?

Modern units are much quieter than many people expect. During planning we look at distance to windows and neighbours, sound power levels of the unit and local rules. We choose the position and, if needed, sound insulation so that typical operation is comparable to a quiet conversation – not a loud fan.

Will my monthly costs really be lower than with my gas boiler?

That depends on three things: the efficiency of the heat pump, how well your building holds heat, and your electricity tariff. We calculate a realistic seasonal efficiency (SCOP) for your system and compare expected annual costs to your current gas or oil consumption – so you see numbers, not marketing promises.

How do subsidies work and do you help with the process?

Yes, we guide you through the subsidy process and tell you in advance which programmes are realistic for your case. We structure our offer in a way that matches the requirements, so you can submit the application with correct, itemised information.

Who carries out the installation – your own team or subcontractors?

Your project is planned and installed by our own team and long-term partner trades, not anonymous subcontractor chains. You know who is responsible on site and who to talk to if you have questions later.
